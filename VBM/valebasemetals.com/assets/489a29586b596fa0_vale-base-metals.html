About us

We are VBM, a global supplier of critical minerals for today’s needs and tomorrow’s transformation. With industry-leading copper, nickel and cobalt resources in Canada, Brazil and Indonesia, we provide a secure and responsible supply to customers around the world. Our purpose is clear: to provide mineral security to a world in transition. Our success is built on agility, resilience and cost-competitiveness, supported by a workforce empowered to innovate and consistently deliver results. With a vast endowment of copper and nickel, established infrastructure and a rich operating history, we are uniquely positioned to meet the growing demand for critical minerals. Critical today. Accelerating tomorrow. - Vale Base Metals Limited is based in London, United Kingdom, with its global operations centre in Toronto, Canada. Vale Base Metals is 90 per cent owned by Vale S.A. (VALE) and 10 per cent by Manara Minerals Investment Company.

    Reliable access to critical minerals starts with strong, long-life assets. In Newfoundland and Labrador, Voisey’s Bay is part of Vale Base Metals’ (VBM) integrated nickel-copper-cobalt production portfolio. It connects mining and processing in Labrador with the Long Harbour refinery in Newfoundland. Long Harbour was built in 2014 and processes nickel concentrate into high-purity (Class I) nickel products. Together, Voisey’s Bay and Long Harbour form one of the lowest carbon-intense nickel production systems in the world, and they are a great example of VBM’s vertically integrated operations. Operating since 2005, Voisey’s Bay is a multiple recipient of the John T. Ryan Award as one of the safest mines in Canada. It is also one of the highest-grade magmatic nickel sulphide districts in the world, with a mine life estimated to run until at least 2039. Voisey’s Bay is driving growth in a staged approach: ➡️Short term: Following the successful transition to underground mining, Voisey’s Bay is focused on achieving steady-state underground performance and cost competitiveness. ➡️Medium term: Advancing a pre-feasibility study to expand mill throughput from 2.5 to 3.8 million tonnes per annum. ➡️Long term: Exploration has the potential to significantly extend mine life, with orebodies open at depth; ongoing drilling supports further growth.
