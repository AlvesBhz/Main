Vale Base MetalsOur ProductsNickel

Nickel has enabled industrial innovation for hundreds of years and remains one of the most versatile and performance-critical metals in the world.

VBM Nickel

At Vale Base Metals, we produce one of the world’s most diverse portfolios of refined nickel products—serving critical industries across alloying, plating, battery, powder metallurgy and chemical applications.

Our operations span Canada, the U.K., Japan, and Brazil, enabling us to deliver high-purity, low-carbon footprint nickel products with the flexibility and reliability our customers demand.

Nickel production is anchored by four world-class refineries:

Copper Cliff (Canada)
Operational since 1973, producing pellets and powders.

Long Harbour (Canada)
Opened in 2014, specializing in electrolytic rounds.

Clydach (U.K.)
In operation since 1902, producing pellets and powders.

Matsusaka (Japan) 
Opened in 1967, producing Tonimet® briquettes for stainless steel.

Facts about VBM Nickel 

Our Clydach refinery was responsible for 100% of all refined nickel produced in the UK in 2024. 

Over five decades of operation, our Copper Cliff Refinery has produced more than 5.4 billion pounds of nickel.

The Clydach Refinery, built in 1902 by Ludwig Mond—the inventor of the carbonyl process—is the birthplace of commercial carbonyl nickel refining and remains one of the largest and most versatile nickel refineries in Europe.

Opened in 1961, our Thompson operation in Manitoba was the first integrated nickel mining, smelting, and refining complex in the Western world.

Nickel has outstanding physical and chemical properties, which make it essential in a myriad of product applications.

Properties & Benefits 

Nickel is corrosion-resistant, heat-tolerant, and highly durable. It is rarely used in pure form and is typically alloyed with metals like iron, chromium, and copper to enhance strength and performance.

Applications 

Nickel is used in:

  • Stainless steel production: Nickel enhances corrosion resistance and strength, making stainless steel ideal for kitchenware, architecture and industrial equipment.
  • Rechargeable batteries: A key component in nickel-metal hydride and lithium-ion batteries, supporting electric vehicles, consumer electronics and renewable energy storage systems.
  • High-performance alloys: Used in aerospace, power generation and chemical processing, nickel-based alloys withstand extreme temperatures and harsh environments.
  • Electroplating and surface finishing: Provides a durable, attractive coating for automotive parts, tools and consumer goods, improving wear resistance and appearance.

Global Demand Trends  

  • Nickel is the sixth-most used industrial metal in the world, after iron, aluminum, copper, zinc and lead.
  • Over 65% of nickel demand is driven by stainless steel production.
  • A growing key use of nickel is in the production of lithium-ion batteries for energy storage applications, such as electric and hybrid vehicles, grid storage, and electronics, which accounted for 14% of total primary nickel demand in 2024.
